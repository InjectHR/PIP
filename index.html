Six-Step PIP Flow

Use the overview image as the anchor, then work through each stage with clear expectations, consistent documentation, and timely review points.

1

Meeting one

List the areas for improvement and set consequences of underperformance.

2

Meeting two

Discuss progress and issue a warning if expectations have not been met.

3

Meeting three

Discuss progress and extend the PIP where performance expectations are met.

4

Meeting four

Discuss progress and extend the PIP where expectations continue to be met.

5

Meeting five

Assess progress and issue a warning if expectations have not been met.

6

Meeting six

Terminate based on continued underperformance only after internal checks and process requirements are complete.

Before Each Decision

Pause at each decision point to make sure the process is fair, evidence-based, and aligned with internal requirements.

Evidence

Check that concerns, expectations, support offered, and progress are documented in specific terms.

Communication

Confirm the employee understands the required improvement, review dates, and possible consequences.

Review

Seek the appropriate HR, leadership, or legal review before warnings, extensions, or termination decisions.

This toolkit is a practical HR aid and should be checked against current company policy and legal guidance before use.
